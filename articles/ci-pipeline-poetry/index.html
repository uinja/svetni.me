CI Pipeline Poetry

Written by Miss Baker

Roses are red,
Tests are green,
One flaky suite,
Breaks every build machine.

I. The Pipeline (A Sonnet)

Shall I compare thee to a stable build?
Thou art more flaky and more temperamental.
The green checkmark today may yet be killed
By test conditions subtly incremental.

Sometime too fast the lint step doth run clear,
And often is slow Docker cache denied;
And every build from random flakiness feared,
By chance, or nature's curse, unspecified.

But thy eternal artifact shall not fade,
Nor lose possession of that SHA thou ow'st;
Step 7 shall succeed, or not be paid,
When in eternal CI our artifact goes't.

So long as builds run, or jobs have eyes to see,
So long lives this, and this gives life to thee.

II. The Pipeline Log

Actual entries from our CI over one week:

  • Step 8 failed while step 8 succeeded.
  • Build succeeded. 2 tests failed.
  • Artifact uploaded successfully. Artifact not found.
  • Job timed out after 4 minutes. Job duration: 3 minutes 58 seconds.
  • Connection to test database failed. 47 tests passed.
  • Deploy skipped (no changes). 14 files changed.

These logs are factually incorrect in ways that are extremely interesting.

III. The Flaky Test

We have one test that fails approximately 30% of the time.
The test is called should handle concurrent requests correctly.
It has been flaky for seven months.
We have investigated it four times.

Current mitigation: retry up to 3 times.

The retry strategy means the test sometimes takes 12 minutes.
During those 12 minutes, nobody merges anything.
The pipeline is a consensus mechanism. We are all waiting.

IV. The Resolution

Last Tuesday, someone deleted the flaky test.

They didn't fix it. They deleted it.

CI is now 11 minutes faster on average.
The functionality it tested remains in production.
The functionality appears to work.

We have added "write replacement test" to the backlog.
It is labeled priority: medium.
Medium means Q4.
Q4 is a long way away.
