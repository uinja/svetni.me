Negotiating Tech Debt: A Delicate Dance

Written by A dev

Tech debt is like that houseplant you keep meaning to water.
Eventually it dies, but somehow you're still watering it.

The Anatomy of a Tech Debt Conversation

Every tech debt negotiation follows the same five-act structure, discovered independently by engineers at every company since approximately 1992.

Act I: The Discovery

"This service has a known race condition that we patched in 2020 with a 500ms sleep. The sleep is still there."

Act II: The Pitch

"We should fix this properly. It'll take about a sprint."

Act III: The Negotiation

"Can we scope it down?"
"We could fix just the race condition and leave the sleep."
"So... fix nothing?"
"Ship the feature first, add this to the backlog."

Act IV: The Backlog
The ticket is created. It is labeled tech-debt, given no story points, assigned to no one, and added to a column called "Future Work."

"Future Work" currently has 94 items. The oldest is from 2019. It involves jQuery.

Act V: The Return
Thirteen months later, a new engineer asks why there's a 500ms sleep in the auth flow.

A Field Guide to Tech Debt Euphemisms

What management says What it means
"It works though, right?" We are not fixing it
"Can we do it incrementally?" We will do none of it
"Let's add it to the backlog" It will die in the backlog
"Technical investment" Tech debt
"Legacy system" Tech debt that is now infrastructure
"Well-understood codebase" Tech debt that everyone has given up on
"Organic growth" Nobody planned anything

The True Cost

Our payment processor integration was built in a weekend in 2017.
It has been "temporary" for seven years.
Three engineers have had breakdown moments related to it.
Two have left the company.
One now works at the payment processor and sends us occasional apologetic emails.

We once estimated a full rewrite at six weeks.
Management approved four.
We shipped the feature instead and patched around the integration.
The patch is now also tech debt.

My Prediction

The 500ms sleep will be in the codebase in five years.
Somebody will write a Medium post about it.
The post will have 2,000 claps.

I am betting the over.
