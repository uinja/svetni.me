The 9 AM Standup: A Daily Ritual of Suffering

Written by A dev

Standup meetings: where time goes to die, one person at a time.

9:00 AM sharp, team gathers. Groggy. Caffeinated. Eager for this to end.

The Format

Three questions. Classic. Elegant. Theoretically fifteen minutes.

  1. What did you do yesterday?
  2. What are you doing today?
  3. Any blockers?

These three questions have been answered approximately 4,000 times by our team collectively. We have learned nothing new from them since Q3 of last year.

A Transcript, Reconstructed

Developer 1: "Yesterday I worked on the auth service. Today I'm continuing on the auth service. No blockers."
(Translation: I have been on the auth service for three weeks. The auth service is a sentient labyrinth. I am lost inside it. I dare not say so publicly.)

Developer 2: "I'm blocked on the design mockups."
(Translation: The mockups were requested two weeks ago. No one will acknowledge this.)

Developer 3: [audible pause] "Yeah, uh... same as yesterday, kind of."
(Translation: I have not yet looked at my tasks this morning. I opened Spotify instead. I regret nothing.)

Scrum Master: "Great! Anything else?"
(Translation: Please say no. Please. I have four more standups today.)

The 11-Minute Tangent

At 9:08 AM, someone casually mentions the API endpoint naming convention.

This is a mistake.

By 9:19 AM, we are now in a deep discussion about REST semantics that could — and should — be a separate meeting, Slack thread, or strongly worded email to nobody.

The Scrum Master has muted themselves.

The Action Items That Aren't

We adjourned at 9:41 AM. Key decisions:

  • Naming conventions: "Let's take this offline" (nobody will schedule this meeting)
  • Design mockups: "Following up on this" (no followup will occur until next standup reveals the same blocker)
  • The auth service: still in progress (it always will be)

Reform Attempts

Last quarter, a developer proposed async standups via a bot. We had a 45-minute meeting to discuss the proposal. The meeting had no clear outcome.

The bot proposal is now in the backlog.

Standup continues at 9:00 AM.
Please mute yourselves until called upon.
Thank you.
