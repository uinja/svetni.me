## Text from Original Article (Summary)
> Mythos is a “frontier AI model”... what’s particularly significant about Mythos is the system it’s embedded within. Understanding this distinction is key... Software security has become a speed race across four stages: detection, verification, coordination, and patch propagation. Open ecosystems distribute these across a community.
> 
> Key arguments for openness:
> - **Leveling the playing field:** Narrowing the gap between attackers and defenders.
> - **Ending proprietary obscurity:** Reverse engineering of closed binaries is becoming easier with AI.
> - **Human-in-the-loop:** Meaningful only if humans can "see into the loop" via open agent scaffolding.
> - **In-house security:** High-stakes orgs can run open systems privately on their own infrastructure.

## Fact Synthesis & Notes
- **Core Message:** The security of AI systems (like Mythos) depends on the surrounding "scaffolding," not just the model. Open source is essential for defense to keep up with AI-enabled attackers.
- **Key Concepts:**
    - **System Recipe:** Compute + Data + Scaffolding + Speed + Autonomy.
    - **Semi-autonomous Agents:** Prespecified actions with human approval as the "sweet spot."
    - **Capability Asymmetry:** Open tools prevent capabilities from being concentrated in few hands.
- **Editorial Strategy:** Focus on the importance of "open scaffolding" and "semi-autonomy." Contrast with the risks of "proprietary obscurity."
