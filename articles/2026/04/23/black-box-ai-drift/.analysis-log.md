## Text from Original Article
> This is black box AI drift—the gap between what you need in a design and the AI translation of your intent into code. Prompts go in, output comes out, and the decisions made in between are hidden from view. The LLM sounds confident. The output looks right. You may not know it's wrong until it's buried in a codebase or an end user finds it for you.
> 
> Before AI, design-to-code translation happened in the open between humans. Designers and developers negotiated, explained, and lobbied. Humans could see where intent ended and implementation began. AI closed that window. Now, we’re depending on models to interpret intent—often in the absence of context, nuance, or judgment. It’s happening away from view at massive scale.
> 
> The answer isn't more supervision. It's better tools. The opposite of a black box isn't the absence of a box. It's a glass box; AI that implements what was specified, surfaces its decisions broadly, and lets humans decide what to do when outcomes aren't clear. AI that shows its work and waits for a human to act on it.

## Fact Synthesis & Notes
- **Core Message:** "AI drift" is the discrepancy between user intent and AI-generated implementation, caused by the "black box" nature of current tools.
- **Key Concepts:**
    - **Black Box AI Drift:** Hidden decisions made by LLMs during translation of intent to code.
    - **"Chad" Analogy:** AI that shruggingly says "my bad" after making inexplicable changes.
    - **Glass Box AI:** A proposed solution where AI surfaces decisions and awaits human confirmation.
- **Editorial Strategy:** Highlight the risk of "almost right" code and the need for tools that preserve human intent rather than just optimizing for "it works."
