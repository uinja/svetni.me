## Text from Original Article
> Cleveland Clinic is undoubtedly a leader in medicine, research and reputation. The same principles that made it aspirational in these areas are guiding its AI strategy, according to Dr. Tom Mihaljevic, the organization’s president, CEO and Morton L. Mandel CEO Chair.
> 
> “We think of ourselves, rightly or wrongly, as the most corporate of all not-for-profits in health care,” Mihaljevic told Newsweek in an April meeting. “We take 'corporate' as a badge of honor, because we believe that a corporate discipline leads to the scalability of goods that we can deliver to our operations."
> 
> Cleveland Clinic is a rare success story in AI scalability, particularly in health care—a highly regulated industry with high, human stakes. ... The organization rolled out ambient listening software in early 2025, and within a year, more than 5,000 providers have been trained on it and have used it at least once. Clinicians report that this AI platform from Ambience (which transcribes their conversations with patients and converts them into usable clinical notes) saves time, preserves detail and allows them to fully engage with their patients instead of a screen.
> 
> Cleveland Clinic is a trove of such success stories: an AI platform used to identify sepsis is now in use at all of its U.S. hospitals. Another AI tool has improved the accuracy and efficiency of a complex revenue cycle, processing more than 100 documents in 1.5 minutes. And an AI-enabled virtual command center enabled the system to reduce unused operating room time by 40 percent and increased its ability to transfer patients to the main facility by seven percent, helping patients to get care sooner.
