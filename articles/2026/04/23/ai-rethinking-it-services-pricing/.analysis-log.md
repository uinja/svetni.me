- **Author:** Adam Mills
- **Published:** 2026-04-23

## Text from Original Article
> The way IT services firms make money is starting to drift out of sync with the way they deliver work.
> 
> For years, companies have priced software development and related services according to how many people are assigned to a project and how long they spend on it, because effort and results tended to move together. More engineers meant more code, more progress and, in turn, more value.
> 
> That relationship is beginning to break down. As AI begins to take on more of the execution itself, the amount a small team can produce has expanded sharply. Work that once depended on large teams can now be handled by far smaller groups working alongside automated systems, compressing timelines and changing how work gets done.
> 
> “The model no longer reflects how value is created,” Abby Kearns, CEO of ActiveState, a software supply chain security company, told Newsweek. AI, she added, is reshaping the relationship between effort and output in ways that traditional pricing approaches struggle to capture.
> 
> “A headcount-based model assumes that value scales with effort, but AI fundamentally challenges that premise,” Rajesh Nambiar, president of NASSCOM, the Indian IT industry trade association, told Newsweek.

## Fact Synthesis & Notes
- **Core Message:** AI is dismantling the traditional "billable hours" and headcount-based pricing models in IT services.
- **Key Facts:**
    - Traditional model: Price = (Number of People) x (Time Spent).
    - AI shift: Small teams + AI automation = High output.
    - Transition to output-based (results delivered) or outcome-based (business success) pricing is accelerating.
    - Major industry figures (CEO of ActiveState, President of NASSCOM, former Cognizant CEO) agree the linear relationship between people and production is gone.
- **Editorial Strategy:** Focus on the "linear relationship" being broken by AI. Highlight the shift from labor arbitrage to value-driven models.
