## Fact Synthesis & Notes
- **Core Message:** 10x Science, a startup founded by Stanford researchers, raised $4.8M to solve the "characterization bottleneck" in AI-driven drug discovery. While AI can predict protein structures, measuring and verifying them (characterization) is slow and requires mass spectrometry expertise. 10x Science uses AI agents to automate and speed up this process.
- **Key Facts:**
    - Seed Round: $4.8 million.
    - Investors: Initialized Capital (lead), Y Combinator, Civilization Ventures, Founder Factor.
    - Founders: David Roberts, Andrew Reiter, Vishnu Tejas.
    - Scientific Pedigree: Founders worked in Nobel laureate Dr. Carolyn Bertozzi's Stanford lab.
    - Technology: AI-native platform for mass spectrometry data interpretation.
    - Customers: Working with major pharma, academic researchers, and chemical analysis firms (e.g., Rilas Technologies).
- **Editorial Strategy:** Focus on the transition from structure prediction (AlphaFold era) to the practical implementation and measurement bottleneck. Highlight the Bulgarian context of AI in science.
