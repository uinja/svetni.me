# Analysis Log - MIT Teaching AI Uncertainty

**Original Article:** [Teaching AI models to say “I’m not sure”](https://news.mit.edu/2026/teaching-ai-models-to-say-im-not-sure-0422)

## Fact Synthesis & Notes
- **Core Message:** Researchers from MIT CSAIL have developed a training method called RLCR (Reinforcement Learning with Calibration Rewards) that teaches AI models to quantify their own uncertainty. Current models (like OpenAI's o1) are often overconfident because they are rewarded only for correct answers. RLCR uses a "Brier score" to reward models for providing accurate confidence estimates alongside their answers.
- **Key Facts:**
    - Method: RLCR (Reinforcement Learning with Calibration Rewards).
    - Technical mechanism: Incorporates a Brier score into the reward function to penalize the gap between stated confidence and actual accuracy.
    - Result: Up to 90% reduction in calibration error while maintaining or improving accuracy.
    - Scope: Tested on a 7-billion-parameter model across various benchmarks (QA and math).
    - Authors: Mehul Damani, Isha Puri, Stewart Slocum, Idan Shenfeld, Leshem Choshen, Jacob Andreas, and Yoon Kim.
    - Context: To be presented at the International Conference on Learning Representations (ICLR) 2026.
    - Significance: Critical for high-stakes applications like medicine, law, and finance where overconfidence can be dangerous.
- **Editorial Strategy:** Highlight the "AI Honesty" aspect. Explain why current models "hallucinate with confidence" due to existing RL training incentives. Explain the Brier score in simple terms.
