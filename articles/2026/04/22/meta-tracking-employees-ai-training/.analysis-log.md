## Fact Synthesis & Notes
- **Core Message:** Meta is implementing a new tool called "Model Capability Initiative" (MCI) to record employee keystrokes, mouse movements, and UI interactions. The data will be used to train AI agents to perform computer tasks autonomously by mimicking human behavior.
- **Key Facts:**
    - Tool name: Model Capability Initiative (MCI).
    - Data recorded: Keystrokes, mouse clicks, dropdown navigation, UI interactions.
    - Official justification: AI agents need real-world examples of how people use computers to solve everyday tasks.
    - Employee sentiment: "Dystopian," "obsession with AI." Concerns are heightened by recent layoffs (2,000 this year) and a hiring freeze (listings dropped from 800 to 7).
    - Financial context: Meta spending $140bn on AI in 2026.
    - Recent AI milestones: Launch of "Muse Spark" from Meta Superintelligence Labs; $14bn investment in Scale AI.
- **Editorial Strategy:** Synthesize the technical necessity of "observational learning" for agents (from TechCrunch/Meta statement) with the human impact and corporate climate at Meta (from BBC).
