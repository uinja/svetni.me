## Core Facts & Synthesis
- **Core Update:** Introduction of "HyperAgents" by Meta and academic researchers.
- **Problem:** Current self-improving AI is limited to coding (like Sakana AI's DGM) because evaluation and modification are coding tasks.
- **Innovation:** HyperAgents fuse "task agent" and "meta agent" into a single, self-referential program that can improve its own self-improvement logic ("metacognitive self-modification").
- **Benchmarks:** Tested on Polyglot (coding), paper review, robotics (quadruped reward model), and math grading.
- **Key Result:** Outperformed domain-specific baselines in non-coding tasks and successfully transferred meta-skills to unseen domains (math grading).
- **Emergent Behaviors:** Independently invented memory tools, performance trackers, and compute-budget awareness.
- **Safety:** Use of sandboxed environments and human-defined correctness checks.
