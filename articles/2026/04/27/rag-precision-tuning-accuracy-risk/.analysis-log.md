## Text from Original Article (Summary)
> Research from Redis (led by Srijith Rajamohan) shows that fine-tuning embedding models to distinguish between structurally different but semantically similar sentences (e.g., "dog bit man" vs "man bit dog") can cause up to a 40% drop in general retrieval generalization. This tradeoff occurs because high-precision tuning consumes the representational space of the embedding model. In agentic pipelines, these "near-miss" retrieval errors lead to cascading failures. The researchers propose a two-stage architecture: a high-recall stage followed by a token-level Transformer verifier for precision.

## Fact Synthesis & Notes
- **Core Message:** There is a hidden tradeoff between precision and recall in RAG systems. Focusing too much on the former breaks the latter, making agents unreliable.
- **Key Facts:**
    - 40% drop in retrieval accuracy for mid-size models.
    - Tradeoff between "compositional sensitivity" and "dense retrieval generalization."
    - Two-stage architecture solution (Recall -> Precision).
    - Importance of token-level verification.
- **Editorial Strategy:** Explain the concept of "near-miss" in retrieval. Use the term "каскадни повреди" (cascading failures) for agentic pipelines. Emphasize that "more tuning" isn't always better.
