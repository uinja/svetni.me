The Meeting That Could Have Been an Email

Written by Miss Baker

Agenda: decide between two button labels.
Duration: 63 minutes.
Outcome: "Let's revisit next week."

The Setup

The button says "Submit." It has said "Submit" for four years. Nobody noticed.

Then someone noticed.

A Slack thread began. The thread grew to 47 messages. Two people quoted articles. One person linked a Nielsen Norman Group report. Someone suggested "Send." Someone else suggested "Continue."

At message 47, someone said: "Let's get in a room."

The Meeting: A Minute-by-Minute Account

00:00 — Meeting starts. Eleven people attend. It is not clear why eleven people attend.

03:00 — Someone shares a 12-slide deck titled "Button Nomenclature: A Strategic Framework." The deck has existed since yesterday. It contains three bullet points repeated in different fonts.

08:00 — Discussion of what "Submit" implies legally. Legal is not present. Someone offers to summarize what legal might say.

14:00 — "Send" proposed again. Objection: "Send implies email." Counter-objection: "Users think of all transmission as sending." Eleven minutes pass.

27:00 — Introduction of "Confirm." Brief excitement. Someone remembers a study that found "Confirm" increases user anxiety. No one can find the study. We proceed as though we have.

35:00 — Side debate about whether the button color also needs to change. This is not on the agenda. It is tabled in a way that does not feel like tabling.

43:00 — Three references to "industry best practices." No specific industry. No specific practice.

57:00 — Straw poll: Submit: 3 votes. Send: 2 votes. Continue: 3 votes. Proceed: 1 vote. Absentia: 2 votes (they left).

63:00 — Outcome: "Let's revisit this next week with more data."

The Resolution

The meeting recurred three times over four weeks.

On the fourth week, a designer quietly changed the button to "Save & Continue" and deployed it to 5% of users.

No one noticed.
No one complained.
The A/B test ran for two weeks.
Both variants performed identically.

The button now says "Submit." It has always said "Submit." Nobody talks about this anymore.
