Retro Action Items Forever

Written by A dev

In every retrospective we create action items.
In every sprint we forget them creatively.

The Retrospective Format

We use the classic three-column format:

  • What went well
  • What didn't go well
  • What can we improve

The "what went well" column is helpful for morale.
The "what didn't go well" column is helpful for honesty.
The "what can we improve" column generates action items.

Action items are where hope goes to live briefly before becoming a backlog ticket.

Action Item Hall of Fame

"Improve onboarding documentation"
Created: 14 months ago.
Owner: assigned to the team (nobody).
Status: The onboarding doc gained two paragraphs. Both were added by new hires trying to figure out onboarding.

"Reduce context switching"
Created: 11 months ago.
Owner: everyone.
Status: We have more meetings now.

"Define our definition of done"
The definition of done is not done. The irony has been noted. It has been noted repeatedly. It has never been resolved.

"Better estimation practices"
Created: 9 months ago.
Status: We ran one planning poker session, found it took longer than the estimates it produced, and quietly returned to gut feel.

"Address fragile E2E tests"
Created: 6 months ago.
Status: We added a retry. The tests are still fragile. We added a second retry.

"Cross-team communication"
Created: 5 months ago.
Owner: everyone.
Status: Slack is very active. Whether this constitutes communication is philosophically contested.

The Closure Strategy

Last quarter, we cleaned up old action items by:

  1. Closing anything older than 6 months with the label stale
  2. Merging duplicates ("improve testing" appeared seven times)
  3. Renaming items to sound more complete ("documentation gap" became "documentation reviewed")

This was effective for the dashboard.
The underlying issues were not reviewed.

Emerging Best Practice

We have started limiting retros to three action items maximum.

This has worked well. Three items are more likely to be addressed than seventeen.

We still generate seventeen per retro.
We pick the top three.
The other fourteen go into the backlog.

In the backlog, they join the fourteen from last sprint's retro.
And the fourteen from the sprint before that.

The backlog of retro items is now longer than the feature backlog.
We have not yet had a retro about this.
